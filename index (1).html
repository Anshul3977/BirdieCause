"The developer has had major financial and debt issues."+
The capital structure has been stabilized via a $1.7B construction debt extension led by JPMorgan Chase. The asset is cross-collateralized with Mall of America and West Edmonton Mall. As a tenant, you're investing in the footfall and the consumer — not the developer's historical construction debt. This stabilized phase is the optimal time to negotiate favorable tenant improvement allowances. §3
"Consumers won't pay for parking at a mall."+
American Dream is not a regional mall — it's a destination resort. Consumer psychology perfectly aligns with paying for parking at a theme park or an arena. With 33,000 spaces, the friction is minimal. This filter actually works in tenants' favor: it prioritizes higher-intent, higher-spending demographics while naturally excluding low-value foot traffic. §3
"It's too hard to reach from Manhattan without a car."+
The target demographic for luxury retail and family entertainment prefers private vehicular travel — perfectly accommodated by 33,000 spaces. The 20 million MSA residents largely reside in car-owning suburban communities. For those in Manhattan, bus routes operate effectively. For UHNWIs, VIP valet is vastly preferable to navigating Fifth Avenue security. §3
"We're shifting our entire budget to e-commerce."+
Generic, undifferentiated retail is dying. Experiential commerce is thriving. With 55% of the footprint dedicated to un-replicable physical entertainment, American Dream is structurally immune to e-commerce erosion. E-commerce CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) is skyrocketing — this property provides profitable, measurable physical customer acquisition at a CPM that outperforms digital for high-intent buyers. §12
"The lease rates and sponsorship CPMs are too high."+
You are not paying for standard retail square footage or generic billboard space.
